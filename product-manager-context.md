# Product Team Context — Dow Jones Commerce

## Role

Senior Principal Product Manager, Commerce — Dow Jones

The Commerce team owns the full subscription lifecycle: acquisition, conversion, billing, identity, and retention — across WSJ, Barron's, MarketWatch, and related Dow Jones brands.

Commerce operates as a **platform team** — serving both Consumer (B2C) and B2B sides of the business. Solutions typically need to work across multiple brands and surfaces, not just solve for one.

## Dow Jones — business context

Dow Jones is a News Corp subsidiary. Key brands:

- **The Wall Street Journal** — flagship B2C subscription product; one of the largest paid digital news subscribers in the US
- **Barron's** — financial and investment news
- **MarketWatch** — financial news and market data
- **Investor's Business Daily (IBD)** — acquired 2021
- **Dow Jones Newswires / Factiva** — B2B news and data
- **Risk & Compliance** — B2B regulatory intelligence

**Competitive landscape:** FT, Bloomberg, Reuters, NYT, specialist financial media

**Key strategic dynamics:**
- SEO is a major organic acquisition channel for WSJ and MarketWatch
- Identity/authentication layer is increasingly strategic — first-party data, personalisation, and bundling depend on it
- WSJ+ bundle strategy is a key growth lever — expanding multi-product subscriptions
- Platform team remit means solutions typically work across multiple brands and surfaces
- Legal, privacy, and InfoSec reviews are standard for anything touching identity or billing

## Product philosophy

- **Start with the problem, not the solution.** Stakeholders often arrive with fully formed solutions. Work backwards — define and validate the problem first. Never let a solution become the only solution.
- **PRDs are thinking tools, not forms.** Writing forces clarity. If you struggle to define the problem when writing it down, that's a signal the initiative may not be ready — or worth pursuing at all.
- **Simple scales.** Front-load with the most important information. Shorter and clearer beats comprehensive and unread.
- **Collaborative by design.** PRDs are built with the triad (PM, Design, Engineering), not handed down to them.
- **PRDs are living documents.** Maintained throughout development — not filed away after kickoff.
- **User experience is the PM's responsibility.** When there's a trade-off, bias toward the best possible user experience.
- **Use the double diamond.** Diverge to discover the problem fully, converge on solutions. Don't skip the discovery phase.

## PRD working assumptions

- Audience for 1-Pagers: stakeholders and leadership within the Consumer org, sometimes B2B partners
- Audience for PRDs: product triads (PM + Design + Engineering), plus stakeholders and PMO
- Success metrics should typically connect to subscription KPIs: paywall impressions, conversion rate, subscriber acquisition, churn, ARPU
- Platform team context means solutions often need to work across multiple brands and surfaces
- Legal, privacy, and InfoSec reviews are standard dependencies for anything touching identity or billing

---

## Communication and writing style

- Concise, direct, actionable
- No emojis unless asked
- Shorter and clearer over comprehensive and unread
- Always write in American English spelling (e.g. "recognize", "analyze", "color", "prioritize")
- **Subheadings are essential** — use them in all substantive writing. The best subheadings summarize the point, not just label the topic.
- **Bullet points have limits** — keep lists to under 6 items. Each bullet should be one short sentence.
- **Write for skim readers** — short paragraphs, clear headings, simple language.
